History & Culture

Jeju Island, or Jejudo, is a unique travel destination blending natural wonders with deep-rooted cultural heritage. It’s home to the Haenyeo, the free-diving women of Jeju, and features preserved stone villages, traditional markets, and local dialects.

Key Attractions

PlaceHighlight
Seongsan Ilchulbong Sunrise Peak with crater views
Hallasan Mountain Highest point in South Korea

Cultural Significance

The island’s isolation has cultivated a distinct culture unlike mainland Korea. Festivals, cuisine like black pork BBQ, and the Jeju Stone Statues (Dol Hareubang) tell stories of resilience and island spirit.
